Mac And Cheese

Description

This is not my recipe for my other favorite dish, mac and cheese.

Ingredients

  • 3 suspiciously large apples (preferably ones that give off an eerie glow)
  • 1 cup of sugar (the sweeter, the sneakier)
  • 2 tablespoons of butter that insists it’s margarine (just let it live its lie)
  • 1 tablespoon of cinnamon, stolen from a friendly witch's pantry
  • 1 cup of flour (freshly harvested from the Garden of Gluten)
  • 1 egg laid by a chicken with a mysterious backstory
  • 1 tablespoon of salt, for balance (but mostly for drama)
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract (if it smells like it's plotting something, you're doing it right)
  • A handful of crushed dreams (optional, but highly recommended)
  • A pie crust that somehow manifested in your fridge overnight

Steps

  1. Summon the pie crust: Take the mysteriously appeared pie crust from your fridge. If it starts whispering, pretend you didn’t hear it.
  2. Charm the apples: Peel and slice the glowing apples, but do it carefully—don’t make eye contact or they might roll off the table in protest.
  3. Sift the flour: Whisper ancient baking incantations while sifting the flour. This won’t help the pie, but it will make you feel important.
  4. Melt the butter: Heat the butter until it begins to question its identity. Stir in sugar and cinnamon, allowing the mixture to simmer until it smells like happiness and chaos.
  5. Whisk the egg: Gently beat the egg while asking it deep existential questions about its journey from chicken to pie.
  6. Mix the dreams: Combine your flour, salt, and crushed dreams in a large bowl. Stir while contemplating life’s strange and unpredictable paths.
  7. Layer the ingredients: Place the apple slices in the pie crust, but do so dramatically. Pour the butter-cinnamon-sugar mixture over the apples like you’re the villain in a baking soap opera.
  8. Bake with hope: Place the pie in the oven at 375°F and bake for 45 minutes. Spend the next 45 minutes trying to convince yourself that the kitchen isn’t haunted.
  9. Cool with suspicion: Let the pie cool, but keep an eye on it. If it starts vibrating or glowing brighter, call a priest.
  10. Serve with confusion: Cut the pie and serve with a scoop of ice cream and a side of existential dread. Enjoy your weirdly delicious creation!
